# App Store Screenshot Guide

## Required Screenshots

Apple App Store requires 5-10 screenshots showing your app's main features.

## Recommended Screenshot Set (in order):

### 1. Dashboard/Home Screen
- Shows: Calorie ring, macro bars, water tracker, streak
- Why: This is your main screen users will see daily
- Tip: Make sure you have some data filled in (calories, water)

### 2. Nutrition Tracking
- Shows: Food log, meal breakdown, nutrition details
- Why: Nutrition tracking is a core feature
- Tip: Add a few meals to show variety (breakfast, lunch, snacks)

### 3. Workout Tracking
- Shows: Workout plan, exercises, active workout
- Why: Shows the workout capabilities
- Tip: Start a workout or show a workout plan with exercises listed

### 4. Progress/Analytics
- Shows: Charts, weight graph, body measurements
- Why: Users love seeing their progress visualized
- Tip: Fill in some historical data to show trending charts

### 5. Food Search/Barcode Scanner (Optional)
- Shows: Food database search or barcode scanning
- Why: Highlights the ease of logging food

## How to Take Screenshots

### On iPhone:
1. Open the app on your iPhone
2. Navigate to the screen you want to capture
3. Press: Side Button + Volume Up (iPhone X and later)
4. Screenshots are saved to Photos app

### On Android:
1. Open the app on your Android phone
2. Navigate to the screen you want to capture
3. Press: Power + Volume Down
4. Screenshots are saved to Gallery

### Using Simulator (Mac only):
1. Run: npx expo start
2. Press I to open iOS simulator
3. In simulator menu: Device > Screenshot
4. Or press: Cmd + S

## Screenshot Specifications

**Apple App Store:**
- Device sizes needed: 6.7 inch (iPhone 15 Pro Max) and 5.5 inch (iPhone 8 Plus)
- Format: PNG or JPEG
- Minimum: 5 screenshots
- Maximum: 10 screenshots

**Google Play Store:**
- Minimum: 2 screenshots
- Maximum: 8 screenshots
- Format: PNG or JPEG (24-bit, no alpha)
- Dimensions: 1080x1920 or higher (16:9 aspect ratio)

## Tips for Great Screenshots

1. **Use Light Mode** - Screenshots look better in light mode (cleaner, more vibrant)
2. **Populate with Data** - Show the app in use, not empty screens
3. **Hide Personal Info** - Use a demo account, not your real name/email
4. **Clean Status Bar** - Full battery, strong signal, 9:41 AM (iOS standard)
5. **Landscape Not Needed** - Only portrait orientation required

## Where to Upload

- Save screenshots to a folder: screenshots/
- Upload to App Store Connect when creating your app listing
- Upload to Google Play Console for Android
